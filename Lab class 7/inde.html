Dhaka is the capital division and administrative heart of Bangladesh. It’s the most populous and economically vibrant region, hosting the capital city, Dhaka. Major industries include textiles, finance, and technology.

Chattogram is home to Bangladesh’s largest seaport. It’s known for its hills, ethnic diversity, and the scenic Cox’s Bazar beach—the world’s longest natural sandy sea beach.

Rajshahi is known as the “Silk City” and is famous for its mangoes, education, and archaeological heritage. It houses sites like Paharpur and Mahasthangarh.

Khulna is known for the Sundarbans mangrove forest and the Royal Bengal Tiger. It also features shipbuilding, shrimp farming, and the Rupsha River.

Barishal is a riverine region often called the "Venice of the East". It’s known for floating guava markets, rice production, and scenic beauty.

Sylhet is known for its tea gardens, Haor wetlands, and spiritual significance. A large number of expatriates in the UK originate from Sylhet.

Rangpur is rich in agriculture and has a strong rural economy. Historical sites like Tajhat Palace add cultural value to the region.

Mymensingh is the newest division of Bangladesh. It is known for Bangladesh Agricultural University and a strong presence in fisheries and farming.
